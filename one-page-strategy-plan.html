↗ Chapter 4 — Your Unique Value Proposition
Specific, unique, benefit-oriented. "We empower [customer] to [outcome] by [how]." Not "great service" — that's table stakes.
↗ Chapter 5 — Ideal Customer
Go beyond demographics. Who are they, what do they want, what keeps them up at night?
↗ Chapter 5 — Where They Are
Where do they go to learn, connect, and solve problems? Online, in person, what they read.

Book it now. Same day each month — treat it as your most important client meeting.
"Having an accountability partner is not a luxury; it is an essential part of your strategic success." — Roy West
↗ Chapter 9 — The Meeting Agenda
5 min
Check-in
One round-robin question: "What was your biggest win from last month, in work or life?" Sets a positive, open tone.
15 min
KPI Review
Review the Simple Scorecard. For Green KPIs: what can we learn? For Red/Amber: what is the root cause — not who is to blame.
45 min
Strategic Discussion
One high-impact topic from the KPI review or One-Page Plan. Deep thinking, not status updates. Ask: "What if we…?" and "Why do you think…?"
15 min
Recalibrate & Actions
Update the One-Page Plan if needed. Assign action items — each with one owner and a deadline. No action without a name and a date.
5 min
Look Ahead
Confirm priorities and key actions for the coming month. Close with an encouraging message tying work back to the Mission and Vision.
One big question to explore at the next meeting. What does your scorecard tell you? What decision needs to be made?
