Strategic reference — Knowledgebase.business
Program Architecture
Decided: 25 May 2026  ·  Roy West  ·  For Evernote reference

The core insight

Knowledgebase.business is not one program. It is three distinct programs sharing a common foundation. Each has a different entry trigger, a different acquisition channel, and a different commercial mechanism. Treating them as a single linear journey was the wrong model.

The old model assumed every client started at Stage 1 and worked through to Stage 5. The new model recognises that business owners enter at the stage they actually need — and some stages are not upgrades at all, they are separate programs.

Survive

Crisis landing spot — not an upgrade, not a milestone
What it is
A standalone program for business owners in crisis right now. Built from Don't Do Nothing. No milestone logic, no upgrade path — you arrive here because you need it, not because you earned it.
The positioning
Most business coaching pretends crisis doesn't exist. We don't. Survive is the program for business owners who want real answers, not Disneyland optimism. Roy has been there. The book exists because he lived it. That's the differentiator.
Entry trigger
A business owner in crisis — cash pressure, key person lost, client disaster, revenue collapse. They find us because they're searching for someone who gets it.
Entry mechanism
Direct. Immediate. No milestones. No waiting. If you need it, you get it now.
CTA on sales page
Get access now — no milestone language anywhere on this section.

Exit

New program on application — not an upgrade, not a buy button
What it is
A dedicated sale preparation program entered by choice, not by achievement. The decision to sell is a separate moment in a business owner's life — it warrants a conversation with Roy, not a checkout flow.
Entry trigger
The business owner decides they want to prepare for sale. Could happen at any point — a client mid-Build, a client who has just joined, or a business owner referred by a broker.
Entry mechanism
Application-based. Talk to Roy. No automated buy button — the first step is a conversation that scopes the engagement properly.
Primary acquisition channel
Business brokers. Brokers refer vendors who need to get sale-ready. The client joins Exit, prepares their business properly, and the broker ends up with a better-prepared, higher-value listing. Both sides win. This is a big push channel — brokers should be actively recruited as referral partners.

What this is not

Survive is not something you upgrade to. A business in crisis does not need to earn access through milestones. The milestone language anywhere near the Survive section undermines the positioning entirely.

Exit is not something you advance to. No one completes their Build milestones and graduates to Exit. The decision to sell is separate from the journey of building. A business owner can enter Exit on day one if they have already decided they want to sell — the Growth Path is irrelevant to that decision.

Scale is not only a destination. An established business that has never been on the Growth Path can enter directly at Scale. The ambition is the entry trigger, not the prior stages.

Key decisions made — 25 May 2026

1
The upgrade path is Start → Build → Scale only. Sequential, milestone-based, earn or buy. Survive and Exit are not on this path.
2
Survive has no milestone language. Immediate access, direct entry, any stage. CTA is "Get access now" — not "unlock" or "earn".
3
Exit has no buy button. Application-based, conversation with Roy, scoped engagement. CTA is "Talk to Roy about Exit".
4
Scale is a dual-entry stage. Natural destination of the Growth Path AND direct entry for ambitious established businesses.
5
Business brokers are the primary Exit acquisition channel. Active recruitment of broker referral partners is a strategic priority — not an afterthought.
