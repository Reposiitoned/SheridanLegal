Your executor/s
An executor is the person who will administer your estate after your death — collecting assets, paying debts, and distributing the estate according to your wishes.
Your executor doesn't need to be a lawyer, but they should be someone you trust completely, who is organised, and ideally younger or the same age as you.

Residuary estate
After specific gifts are distributed and debts paid, the remainder of your estate is the residuary estate.
Most people leave the residuary estate to their partner absolutely, or if the partner predeceases them, equally to their children.
