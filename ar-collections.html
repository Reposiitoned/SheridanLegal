Credit gate policy
Layer 0 — Risk management

New clients over $10,000/month scope: 100% upfront for first 3 months.

Large scopes on 30-day terms: credit check before offering terms.

Offering 30-day terms is offering interest-free credit. Treat it like a lending decision.

The mindset reframe
Layer 1 — Read this with your team before the system goes live
The reframe that makes everything else work

Most businesses treat collecting money as awkward. It feels like chasing. It damages relationships. So they delay, soften the language, and hope the client pays without being asked. This is the single biggest reason AR processes fail.

→
An invoice is not a request. It is a promise your client made when they agreed to the work. You are not asking them for a favour — you are holding them to their commitment.
→
Following up is not chasing. It is a professional obligation. A client who does not pay on time is using your business as an interest-free overdraft. That is not acceptable — and it is not personal.
→
The billing admin runs a system, not a conversation. The system follows the rules. The billing admin does not negotiate, apologise, or delay. They follow the timeline.
→
The Good Cop stays clean. The account manager never chases money. This protects the relationship — and it works because the billing admin holds the line.

The good cop / bad cop split
Layer 2 — Roles. The most common reason AR processes fail: the wrong person is chasing the money.
The rule

The account manager, delivery lead, or Office Manager never chases money. Ever. That's your job — and it's a feature, not a bug.

Role Responsible for Never does
Account Manager / CFO Protects the client relationship. Escalates if client raises billing directly. Chases invoices, negotiates payment, issues reminders
Billing Admin (you) Runs the entire AR process. Issues reminders. Makes Day 21 calls. Escalates at Day 45. Makes delivery decisions, contacts clients about project matters
When a client complains to the Account Manager about a reminder: "Ah yes, our finance system locks things down automatically at Day 45 — I can't override it. Let me get billing@ on this so we can get it sorted and keep things moving."
The escalation timeline
Layer 3 — Process. Day 0 to Day 45. Follow this exactly.
Day 0
Invoice issued
Same day as delivery or milestone. Includes all required details and a one-click payment link. 14-day terms stated clearly.
Day 7
Automated reminder
Friendly tone. Includes payment link. No action required if already paid.
Day 14
Due date email
Invoice now due. Direct payment link. Sent from billing alias — not the account manager.
Day 21 ⚠️
Personal phone call — you make this call
Use the Day 21 script below. Goal: a payment date commitment. Follow up with email same day.
Day 30 🔴
Formal written notice
Interest clause invoked. New work paused. Payment plan offered. Written only — no phone call. Create a paper trail.
Day 45 🔴 STOP WORK
Escalate to debt collector or solicitor
All work stopped. No further direct contact from billing admin. Account manager notified.

Day 21 — Phone call

Opening:

"Hi [Name], it's [your name] from [Business]. I'm just following up on invoice [number] for $[amount] — it was due on [date] and I haven't seen it come through yet. Is there anything on your end that's holding it up?"

If they haven't received it:

"No problem at all — I'll resend it right now while we're on the phone. Can you confirm the best email address to send it to?"

If they say they'll pay next week:

"Great — what day next week should I expect it? I'll make a note so I know when to look for it."

If they push back on the amount:

"I'll flag that with [Account Manager] and get back to you today. In the meantime, is the undisputed portion something we can get processed?"
Day 30 — Formal email
"Dear [Name], Invoice [number] for $[amount] remains outstanding at 30 days. As per our payment terms, interest at [X]% per month has now been applied. New work on your account has been paused pending payment. To arrange immediate payment or discuss a payment plan, please contact me directly at billing@[yourbusiness] or on [phone]. We're keen to resolve this and get things moving again."
When the process meets resistance
Layer 5 — Follow-up strategy. The three most common objections and exactly what to do.
"We've got cash flow issues right now."
Offer a payment plan: 50% now, 50% in 14 days. Get it in writing. Do not extend terms informally — a verbal agreement is not an agreement. Note in the system: this client is a credit risk. Flag for the credit gate on next engagement.
"We're disputing the invoice."
Escalate immediately to Account Manager for the disputed portion only. Request payment of the undisputed portion within 48 hours. Document the dispute. Do not pause the process on the undisputed amount.
"The person who handles invoices is on leave."
Ask who is covering and get their direct contact. Send the invoice directly to the cover person. Do not accept "they'll deal with it when they're back" as a response.
Channel sequence as days increase: Day 7–14: Email only, friendly tone.  ·  Day 21: Phone first, follow up with email same day.  ·  Day 30: Written notice only — no phone call, create a paper trail.  ·  Day 45: Solicitor or debt collector. No further direct contact.

Session review questions
Ask these at every session. The answers tell you whether the system is running or just documented.
  • 1
    What's your current AR days figure, and how has it moved since last session?
    A rising AR days number with a high CEI means clients are slow. A rising number with a low CEI means your process broke down.
  • 2
    Are there any invoices currently at Day 21 or beyond? What's the status?
    This should be on the tip of the billing admin's tongue. If they have to look it up, the process isn't embedded yet.
  • 3
    Has the Good Cop / Bad Cop separation held since last session?
    Watch for signs the account manager has been drawn into chasing. It always starts small — "I'll just send them a quick message."
  • 4
    Are all new clients going through the upfront contract process before work begins?
    The most common shortcut is skipping Layer 0 for "good" clients. This is where most overdue invoices start.
  • 5
    If the billing admin left today, could someone else run this process from Monday?
    This is the succession test. If the answer is no, Layer 7 isn't complete.
  • 6
    What's your biggest cash flow constraint right now, and is AR part of it?
    Connects the KPI to the business outcome. AR improvement should be visible in the cash position within 30–60 days of implementing this system.
Red flags — act on these immediately
If you see any of these at a session, don't move on until they're addressed.
  • 🚩
    AR days above 30 and rising The process has broken down somewhere between Day 7 and Day 21. Review whether reminders are going out and whether the Day 21 call is being made.
  • 🚩
    More than 15% of AR in the 60+ day bucket This indicates systematic process failure, not just slow clients. Trace each invoice and find where the escalation stopped.
  • 🚩
    Account manager is chasing invoices The Good Cop / Bad Cop separation has collapsed. This always damages the client relationship and always needs to be reset immediately.
  • 🚩
    New clients starting without Layer 0 complete The upfront contract process was skipped. The next overdue invoice from this client is almost certain. Address before work continues.
  • 🚩
    CEI below 80% Less than 80% of collectable AR is being collected in the period. This is a serious process failure requiring immediate review of every overdue account.
The Monday morning succession test
Layer 7 — Ask this at every quarterly review

"If the billing admin resigned today and a new person started Monday with only this system and the handover checklist — could they run the full AR Collections process without asking anyone for help?"

If the answer is no, the system is not complete. Layer 7 needs more work before this solution is truly Definitive in this business.
