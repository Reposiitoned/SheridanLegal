The problem
People problems don't fix themselves.
Silence makes them worse.
Most small business owners avoid difficult conversations with their team. Not because they don't care — because they don't have a framework for having them well. So the underperformer stays. The miscommunication festers. The good person leaves because they never felt seen.

1:1 Meeting Framework
The fortnightly 1:1 is the most underused leadership tool in small business. Done well, it surfaces problems before they become crises, builds trust, and develops capability. This tool structures every 1:1 so it's consistently valuable — not a status update dressed as a conversation.
Pre-meeting prompt sent to the team member automatically
Structured agenda — wins, challenges, priorities, development
Action log — captures commitments from both sides
History view — see the pattern of conversations over time
When you need it
The situations this section was built for.
The underperformer you've been avoiding
"I know I need to have the conversation but I don't know how to start it without it becoming a confrontation."
The Performance Conversation Framework structures exactly this conversation — preparation, approach, and what to do after.
The good person who's disengaged
"She's been with us for four years. Lately she seems flat. I don't know what's going on and I'm scared to ask."
The 1:1 framework creates a regular, safe space for exactly this conversation — before it becomes a resignation.

"We've hired three people in two years. None of them lasted 12 months. Something's wrong with our onboarding."

The 1:1 and performance frameworks build team capability and confidence — so decisions start happening without you.
